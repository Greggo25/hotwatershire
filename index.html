Hot Water FAQs — Sutherland Shire

Answers to the most common questions we get from Sutherland Shire homeowners about hot water systems.

Hot water system installation in Sutherland Shire typically costs between $800 and $2,500 depending on the system type (electric, gas, solar, or heat pump), tank size, and complexity of the job. We always provide an upfront fixed quote before any work begins — no surprises.

Yes, in most cases. If you call us before noon, we aim to have a licensed Shire plumber to your property the same day. We understand being without hot water is an emergency and we prioritise these calls.

We service all major brands including Rheem, Rinnai, Dux, Bosch, Chromagen, Vulcan, Everhot, AquaMAX, Thermann, Stiebel Eltron, Saxon, Quantum, Apricus and Wilson Solar. If you have a brand not listed, call us — we service them all.

Electric and gas storage systems typically last 8–12 years. Continuous flow (instantaneous) systems last 15–20 years. Solar and heat pump systems can last 15–20+ years with regular servicing. Sutherland Shire's water quality is generally good for hot water systems, but regular maintenance extends system life significantly.

Yes. NSW homeowners upgrading to an energy-efficient hot water system (heat pump or solar) may be eligible for rebates under the NSW Energy Savings Scheme and federal STC (Small-scale Technology Certificates) rebates. These can reduce the cost of a new system by $300–$1,000+. Ask us about your eligibility when you call.

If your system is under 8 years old, repair is usually cost-effective. If it's over 10–12 years old and showing problems, replacement often makes more economic sense, especially with energy-efficient options and available rebates.
